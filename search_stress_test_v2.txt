⏱️  TIMELINE:
   Simple: Same day to 3 business days if a permit is actually required and the application is complete; unincorporated residential generator work is often handled as no-county-permit work unless floodplain/structural review is triggered
   Complex: 3-7 business days if electrical + gas + structural/floodplain review is required, longer if the address is near a floodplain or the site is inside city limits and must be rerouted to that city’s permit office

📜 LICENSE: Licensed electrician pulls the electrical permit on behalf of the homeowner if the work is in a jurisdiction that requires one; the electrician’s license number goes on the application. For gas piping, a licensed gas plumber or mechanical contractor pulls the gas/mechanical permit on behalf of the homeowner. In unincorporated Harris County, residential generator work often does not require a county permit, but if the property is inside city limits, the permit is still required.

🔍 INSPECTIONS (3 required):
   1. Electrical Final / Generator Connection — Inspector verifies: transfer switch type and listing, feeder/conductor sizing, grounding and bonding, working clearances, disconnecting means, proper labeling, and that the generator is installed per manufacturer instructions and NEC 445/702.
      When: After all wiring is complete, before energizing the standby system
   2. Gas Piping / Pressure Test — Inspector verifies: gas line sizing for total BTU load, shutoff valve location, sediment trap if required, pressure test results, approved materials, and leak-free connections to the generator.
      When: Before covering piping and before the generator is placed into service
   3. Building / Pad or Setback Review — Inspector verifies: concrete pad dimensions, anchorage, equipment elevation if floodplain applies, required setbacks from windows, doors, HVAC returns, and property lines, plus manufacturer-required clearances for combustion air and service access.
      When: Before final placement or before the unit is permanently anchored

💡 PRO TIPS:
   • Confirm the parcel is truly outside city limits before you buy the generator or open any permit application — Harris County unincorporated rules are very different from Houston and the other cities inside the county
   • Get the electrical and gas paperwork ready together so you can avoid a second trip if the site turns out to be inside a city jurisdiction
   • Check setback distances from windows, doors, and fresh-air intakes before setting the pad — moving a generator after the fact is expensive

⚠️  AVOID:
   • Assuming unincorporated Harris County means no rules at all — if the property is actually inside Houston, Pasadena, Katy, Spring, Humble, or another city, the city permit rules apply instead
   • Installing the generator too close to windows, doors, or HVAC return air intakes — this is a common carbon monoxide and setback violation
   • Doing transfer switch work without the electrical permit in a city jurisdiction — that is a major code violation

      When: After all equipment is installed and labeled, before utility permission to operate

💡 PRO TIPS:

⚠️  AVOID:
   • Assuming permit approval means the system can be turned on — utility permission to operate (PTO) is separate and usually takes longer
   • Missing required fire access pathways and roof setbacks — panels cannot cover the full roof plane; inspectors commonly fail arrays that block ridge/eave access
   • Submitting without structural attachment details or roof load verification — solar adds dead load and uplift forces that must be shown on the plans
