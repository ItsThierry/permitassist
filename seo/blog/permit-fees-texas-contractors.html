Permit Fees for Texas Contractors (2025 Breakdown)

For any contracting business in Texas, accurately estimating job costs is crucial for profitability. A key variable in this calculation is the permit fee, which can differ significantly from one city to another. This guide provides an estimated breakdown of common permit fees for residential work in major Texas metropolitan areas.

CRITICAL DISCLAIMER: Permit fee schedules are complex and change frequently. This information is for general estimation purposes only and is based on data from early 2025. ALWAYS verify the current fees with the local building department before providing a final quote to a customer.

HVAC Permit Fees (Residential System Replacement)

These fees typically cover the replacement of a standard split system (condenser and air handler/furnace).

City Estimated Permit Fee Notes
Austin $85 - $150 Based on work valuation. Includes technology and processing fees.
Dallas $120 - $140 Often a flat fee for the first system, with a reduced fee for additional systems.
Houston $90 - $120 Usually a base fee plus a per-equipment charge.
San Antonio $75 - $110 Fee structure is often based on the number of appliances being installed.
Fort Worth $100 - $130 Typically a flat fee for a standard replacement.

Electrical Permit Fees (Panel Upgrade)

Estimates for upgrading a main service panel (e.g., 100A to 200A), which is one of the most common major electrical jobs.

City Estimated Permit Fee Notes
Austin $150 - $250 Fee is based on valuation and can be higher than other cities.
Dallas $100 - $175 Generally based on a "new service" fee category.
Houston $125 - $200 Includes base permit fee plus charges for the new service equipment.
San Antonio $100 - $150 Fees are often tiered based on the amperage of the new service.
Fort Worth $110 - $180 Includes inspection fees for the new service installation.

Plumbing Permit Fees (Water Heater Replacement)

Fees for replacing a standard tank-style water heater (gas or electric) in the same location.

City Estimated Permit Fee Notes
Austin $80 - $120 Based on work valuation, which includes the cost of the unit and labor.
Dallas $90 - $130 Often a flat fee for a single appliance replacement.
Houston $85 - $115 Comprised of a base permit fee plus a specific charge for the water heater.
San Antonio $70 - $100 Tends to be on the lower end for this type of work.
Fort Worth $80 - $120 A straightforward flat fee is common for appliance replacements.

Factors That Influence Permit Costs

Remember that the final cost can be affected by several factors:

  • Plan Review Fees: For complex jobs requiring detailed plans, an additional fee for plan review is almost always charged.
  • Technology/Portal Fees: Most cities now add a small percentage or flat fee to cover the costs of their online permitting systems.
  • Re-inspection Fees: If your work fails the first inspection, you will have to pay a re-inspection fee, which can be $50-$100 or more.
  • After-Hours or Expedited Fees: If you need an inspection outside of normal business hours, expect to pay a premium.
  • Penalties: Starting work without a permit and getting caught will often result in the fee being doubled.

Building permit fees into your quotes accurately is a sign of a professional and well-managed contracting business. Using outdated or incorrect fee information can eat into your profits or lead to awkward conversations with clients. Always take the extra five minutes to verify.
