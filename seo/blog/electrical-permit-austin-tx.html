Electrical Permit Requirements in Austin, TX (2025)

For licensed electricians in Austin, pulling the correct permits is a fundamental part of every project. The City of Austin Development Services Department (DSD) has stringent requirements based on the National Electrical Code (NEC) with local amendments. This guide provides a clear roadmap for the electrical permitting process in 2025.

When is an Electrical Permit Required in Austin?

An electrical permit is required for nearly all new electrical work and most modifications to existing systems. The goal is to ensure all work is performed safely by a licensed professional and inspected for code compliance. You must get a permit for:

  • New Electrical Service: Installing new service from the utility, including the meter base and main panel.
  • Service Panel Upgrade or Replacement: Any change-out of a main electrical panel or subpanel.
  • Adding New Circuits: Running new wiring for outlets, switches, or dedicated appliances (e.g., EV chargers, hot tubs).
  • Structural Wiring: Installing wiring within walls, ceilings, or floors for lighting, outlets, or other fixtures.
  • Low-Voltage Systems: In many cases, permits are also required for low-voltage work like fire alarms and security systems.

Work Exempt from an Electrical Permit

The city allows minor electrical work without a permit. This typically includes:

  • Replacing a switch, receptacle, or light fixture with a new one in the same location (no new wiring).
  • Replacing a circuit breaker with one of the same amperage.
  • Minor repairs that do not involve altering the circuit's path or capacity.

If you have to run new wire, alter a circuit, or replace a panel, you need a permit.

The Austin Electrical Permitting Process

Austin's permitting is handled through the Austin Build + Connect (AB+C) online portal. Electrical contractors must be registered with the city and hold a valid Texas Electrical Contractor License (TECL).

1. Application Submission via AB+C Portal

The online application will require specific details:

  • Project address and property owner information.
  • A comprehensive scope of work. Be specific (e.g., "Upgrade 150A main panel to 200A," "Install (1) 240V/50A circuit for EV charger").
  • For larger projects, a plan review may be required, which involves uploading electrical drawings.
  • Your TECL number and master electrician's information.

2. Review and Issuance

Simple, "stand-alone" electrical permits for residential work are often reviewed and issued quickly through the portal. More complex commercial projects or those tied to larger building permits will undergo a more detailed review. Once approved and fees are paid, the permit is issued electronically.

3. Electrical Permit Fees

Fees are calculated based on the scope and valuation of the project. The DSD fee schedule is the official source, but here's a general idea for 2025:

Work Type Estimated Fee
New Single-Family Service/Panel ~$150 - $250
Panel Upgrade (like-for-like location) ~$100 - $175
Adding a Few Circuits ~$80 - $120

Disclaimer: These are estimates. The City of Austin's official fee schedule is the only accurate source for pricing. Fees are subject to change.

Electrical Inspections in Austin

Inspections are mandatory to close out a permit. You may need multiple inspections depending on the project (e.g., a "rough-in" before drywall and a "final" after completion).

Common Inspector Checklist Items:

  • Proper Grounding and Bonding: Correctly installed grounding electrode conductors and bonding of water/gas pipes.
  • Correct Wire Gauge: Wire size must match the circuit breaker's amperage (e.g., 12 AWG for 20A circuits).
  • Arc-Fault/GFCI Protection: AFCI and GFCI breakers or outlets installed where required by the current NEC.
  • Workmanship: Neat and workmanlike installation, proper stapling of wires, and correct box fill calculations.
  • Panel Schedule: The panel directory must be filled out accurately and legibly.
  • Torque Requirements: Lug nuts on breakers and bus bars must be torqued to manufacturer specifications.

The contractor is responsible for scheduling inspections through the AB+C portal and ensuring the site is ready and accessible.

Consequences of Unpermitted Work

Performing electrical work without a permit in Austin is a significant violation. It can result in stop-work orders, fines, and having to remove or redo work. For contractors, it can trigger an investigation by the Texas Department of Licensing and Regulation (TDLR), risking your license. Always prioritize safety and compliance.
