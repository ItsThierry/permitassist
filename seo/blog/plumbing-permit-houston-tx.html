Plumbing Permit Requirements in Houston, TX (2025)

For master plumbers and plumbing contractors in Houston, securing the right permits from the Houston Permitting Center is a routine but critical part of the job. The city enforces the Houston Plumbing Code to protect public health and safety. This 2025 guide details when you need a permit and how to get one.

When is a Plumbing Permit Required in Houston?

A plumbing permit is required for most work that involves altering, installing, or replacing plumbing systems. If you're opening up walls or going under a slab, you almost certainly need a permit. Key examples include:

  • Water Heater Replacement: Installing a new tank or tankless water heater.
  • Sewer Line Repair/Replacement: Any work on the building's drain or sewer lines.
  • Water Service Line Repair/Replacement: Work on the main water supply line to the property.
  • Re-piping: Replacing galvanized or other old water supply lines within a building.
  • New Plumbing Fixtures: Installing a new shower, tub, sink, or toilet where one did not previously exist (involves new drain/water lines).
  • Gas Line Work: Installing or repairing natural gas piping for appliances.

Work Exempt from a Plumbing Permit

Minor repairs and maintenance do not require a permit. The city defines this as "stopping of leaks in drains, water, soil, waste or vent pipe." You generally do not need a permit for:

  • Clearing stoppages or clogs in a drain line.
  • Repairing or replacing a faucet.
  • Replacing a toilet in its existing location (if the flange is not moved).
  • Repairing small, accessible leaks in water pipes.

The exemption ends when you have to cut into a wall, ceiling, or foundation to replace a section of pipe.

The Houston Plumbing Permitting Process

Licensed plumbing contractors must be registered with the City of Houston to pull permits, which is primarily done online.

1. Application and Documentation

When applying through the Houston Permitting Center's online portal, you'll need:

  • The physical address of the job.
  • A clear and concise description of the work. For example, "Replace 40-gallon gas water heater in same location" or "Tunnel under slab to replace 30 ft of cast iron sewer line."
  • For complex jobs like whole-house re-pipes or commercial work, isometric drawings or plans may be required for review.
  • Your Master Plumber's license number and company information.

2. Permit Issuance

For common residential jobs like a water heater replacement, permits are often issued quickly with minimal review. For more extensive work, the application will be routed for plan review, which adds time to the process.

Plumbing Inspections in Houston

Inspections are essential for verifying code compliance. Depending on the job, you may need several inspections:

  • Underground/Under-slab: For any work done below the foundation, before backfilling.
  • Top-out/Rough-in: Before drywall or insulation is installed, to inspect in-wall piping.
  • Final Inspection: After all work is complete and fixtures are set.

What Inspectors Look For:

  • Proper Materials: Use of approved pipes, fittings, and materials per code.
  • Leak-free Connections: Water and gas lines are often pressure-tested. DWV systems are tested with a water head.
  • Correct Slope: Drain lines must have a minimum slope (typically 1/4" per foot).
  • Venting: Every fixture must be properly vented.
  • Safety Devices: Correct installation of temperature and pressure relief valves on water heaters, sediment traps on gas lines, etc.

Scheduling is done online. Ensure the job site is ready and the work is accessible for the inspector.

Why Compliance is Non-Negotiable

Performing plumbing work without a permit in Houston can lead to severe penalties, including stop-work orders, fines, and action against your license by the Texas State Board of Plumbing Examiners (TSBPE). It is a serious risk to your business and public safety. Always pull a permit.
