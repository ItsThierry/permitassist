Do I Need a EV Charger Permit in Mobile?

The short answer: almost always yes. REQUIRED for any new dedicated circuit or hardwired EV charger installation — and Mobile is no exception. The City of Mobile Building Services Division enforces electrical permit requirements for residential and commercial work.

✅ Work That Always Requires a Permit

  • Installing a new 240V dedicated circuit for Level 2 charging
  • Hardwired EVSE (Electric Vehicle Supply Equipment) installation
  • Installing NEMA 14-50 outlet on new circuit
⚠️ Don't skip the permit. Working without a permit in Mobile can result in stop-work orders, fines, failed home sales, and liability if the work causes damage or injury.

🔍 Inspection Requirements

After the permit is issued, Mobile requires inspections at key stages. Do not cover work before it's inspected.

  • Rough-in Inspection: Wire sizing, conduit installation, proper grounding verified before covering.
  • Final Inspection: EVSE unit installed and tested, GFCI functional, circuit labeled in panel.

📝 How to Apply for a EV Charger Permit in Mobile

  1. Verify your contractor is licensed — Licensed Electrician in most states. Homeowners can pull in some states for owner-occupied homes..
  2. Gather required documents — Equipment specs, site plan, load calculations where applicable.
  3. Submit the application — Online at City of Mobile Building Services Division or in person.
  4. Pay the permit fee — $75–$200 (ESTIMATED).
  5. Post the permit — Keep a copy on-site until all inspections pass.
  6. Schedule inspections — Do not cover work until the inspector signs off.

🚫 Common EV Charger Permit Mistakes in Mobile

  • Not checking panel capacity before installing — often triggers unexpected panel upgrade
  • Missing GFCI protection (NEC 625.22 requirement — strictly enforced)
  • Incorrect wire sizing — must be 125% of charger continuous load per NEC 625.42
  • Not pulling a permit — common because homeowners assume plug-in = no permit
