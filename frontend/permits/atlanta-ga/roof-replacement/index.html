6 · Inspections required

Inspection path to close the permit

Plan the inspection sequence before scheduling labor. Closing the permit usually depends on passing the required inspection stages and correcting any inspector notes quickly.

1

Pre-cover / Deck Inspection

Inspector checks roof decking condition, nailing pattern, sheathing replacement areas, rafter/truss repairs, edge framing, and whether any structural damage was exposed after tear-off. If decking is replaced, this inspection is critical before underlayment is covered.

Timing: After tear-off and any deck repairs, before underlayment and shingles are installed

2

Final Roofing Inspection

Inspector verifies completed roof covering, flashing at chimneys/walls/valleys, drip edge, underlayment, ventilation, penetrations, and that the installed material matches the approved scope. They also look for proper cleanup and no exposed fasteners or unfinished penetrations.

Timing: After all roofing work is complete

7 · Common contractor mistakes

Avoid these Atlanta permit delays

PermitIQ flagged the following mistake patterns, rejection patterns, or pro tips for this trade and city. Use them as a final check before submission.

  • Starting tear-off before the permit is issued and posted — Atlanta inspectors will not sign off on work that was started without an active permit
  • Using the wrong permit type — a full roof replacement needs a Building Permit, not a generic repair permit
  • Replacing roof decking without calling for a deck inspection before covering it with underlayment
  • Failing to verify whether the property is inside Atlanta city limits versus unincorporated Fulton County — the permit office changes by jurisdiction
  • Skipping HOA or historic district approval for shingle color, material, or visible roof changes
  • Submitting permit application without verifying current contractor registration status if the contractor is also required to maintain any city-level registration for the project type
  • Pull the permit before any tear-off. In Atlanta, the roof permit is handled electronically, and starting early is the fastest way to get a failed inspection and delay the closeout.
  • If the roof is older and the house was built before 1980, check whether any demolition/renovation documentation is needed for asbestos-related concerns before you start.

A good rule: make the scope, permit type, license information, address, and inspection plan painfully obvious. That lowers the chance that an intake reviewer has to guess what you meant.
