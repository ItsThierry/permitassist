7 · Common contractor mistakes

Avoid these Dallas permit delays

PermitIQ flagged the following mistake patterns, rejection patterns, or pro tips for this trade and city. Use them as a final check before submission.

  • Submitting the job under the wrong permit type — Dallas wants a Plumbing Permit for water heater replacement, not a generic building permit
  • Starting work before the permit is issued and posted — Dallas can issue a stop-work order and require reinspection
  • Understating project valuation in the portal — Dallas uses a permit valuation system and undervaluing can trigger audit or penalty fees
  • Forgetting annual Dallas contractor registration — an expired city registration can silently block permit approval
  • Missing the T&P discharge requirements — the relief line must terminate at an approved location, not into a wall or capped drain
  • Not checking for a closed system — if there is a backflow preventer, an expansion tank is typically required
  • Dallas residential trade permits are often same-day in the online portal, so submit early in the day with the exact water heater model and license number ready.
  • Check your Dallas contractor registration renewal date before starting any permit application — registration lapses are one of the most avoidable rejection reasons.

A good rule: make the scope, permit type, license information, address, and inspection plan painfully obvious. That lowers the chance that an intake reviewer has to guess what you meant.
