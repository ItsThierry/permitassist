6 · Inspections required

Inspection path to close the permit

Plan the inspection sequence before scheduling labor. Closing the permit usually depends on passing the required inspection stages and correcting any inspector notes quickly.

1

Rough-In / Pre-cover Electrical

Inspector checks PV conductor routing, conduit support, equipment grounding and bonding, DC/AC disconnect location, rapid shutdown compliance, correct breaker sizing, and required NEC 690 labels before final concealment.

Timing: Before energizing the system and before any concealed wiring is covered

2

Roof / Structural

Inspector verifies racking attachment into approved framing members, flashing and roof penetration sealing, roof load assumptions, module setback pathways, and that panels do not block required fire access routes.

Timing: After racking is installed but before final sign-off

3

Final Solar Inspection

Inspector checks inverter operation, placards and directory labels, disconnect accessibility, module layout matches approved plans, clear roof access pathways remain open, and the installation is complete and safe for utility approval.

Timing: After all equipment is installed and before requesting utility PTO

7 · Common contractor mistakes

Avoid these Houston permit delays

PermitIQ flagged the following mistake patterns, rejection patterns, or pro tips for this trade and city. Use them as a final check before submission.

  • Assuming permit approval means the system can be turned on — utility permission to operate (PTO) is separate and usually takes longer
  • Missing required fire access pathways and roof setbacks — panels cannot cover the full roof plane and inspectors will fail layouts that block ridge/eave access
  • Under-sizing or mislabeling NEC 690 disconnects, placards, and rapid shutdown markings
  • Not verifying whether the property is inside Houston city limits versus unincorporated Harris County before applying
  • Submitting structural details that do not show roof attachment points or load path for the racking system
  • Starting work before the permit is issued and posted on site
  • Start the utility interconnection application the same day you submit the permit — PTO often takes longer than the city permit
  • Ask the permit office whether SolarAPP+ is available for your exact residential system; if accepted, it can eliminate plan review delays

A good rule: make the scope, permit type, license information, address, and inspection plan painfully obvious. That lowers the chance that an intake reviewer has to guess what you meant.
