6 · Inspections required

Inspection path to close the permit

Plan the inspection sequence before scheduling labor. Closing the permit usually depends on passing the required inspection stages and correcting any inspector notes quickly.

1

Roofing Underlayment / Deck Inspection

Inspector checks roof deck condition, any replaced sheathing, nailing pattern, underlayment type and lap, drip edge, flashing at valleys/chimneys/walls, and whether any dry-in work was done before inspection.

Timing: After tear-off and any deck repair, before underlayment is fully covered by new roofing

2

Final Roofing Inspection

Inspector verifies completed shingle or membrane installation, ridge caps, flashing details, penetrations, vents, edge metal, and that the roof matches the approved scope with no exposed fasteners or unfinished areas.

Timing: After all roofing work is complete

7 · Common contractor mistakes

Avoid these Phoenix permit delays

PermitIQ flagged the following mistake patterns, rejection patterns, or pro tips for this trade and city. Use them as a final check before submission.

  • Starting tear-off before the permit is issued and posted — Phoenix inspectors can refuse final approval if work began without an active permit
  • Submitting without current Phoenix City Contractor Registration — an expired registration will silently block the application
  • Using the wrong permit path instead of Phoenix's combined permit system for a roof replacement
  • Replacing roof decking or changing roofing material without expecting plan review
  • Skipping the deck/underlayment inspection before covering the roof
  • Not checking HOA approval, historic district rules, or neighborhood material/color restrictions before ordering shingles
  • Pull the permit before any tear-off. Phoenix is strict about work starting only after permit issuance.
  • Check your Phoenix contractor registration renewal date before starting the application — registration lapses are a common avoidable rejection reason.

A good rule: make the scope, permit type, license information, address, and inspection plan painfully obvious. That lowers the chance that an intake reviewer has to guess what you meant.
